Converting from C mass to C concentration

Right now, mg.carbon is the mass of charcoal for each sample. To obtain a comparable metric, I need to convert the kmd mg.carbon into charcoal concentration. The equation Dan gave me in an email is the following:

(% C of digested sample * weight of digested sample on filter paper) / mass of soil sample used in digestion = g PyC / g of soil * 1000 / 100 = mg PyC / g of soil

kmd_samples <- sites[which(sites$type == "kmd"),]
kmd_samples$PyC_mg <- (((kmd_samples$C_percent*kmd_samples$digest_weight)/kmd_samples$kmd_starting_weight)*1000)/100

Calculating standards

Notes from 5/9/18 meeting with Dan:

  • I wont really be doing anything with the costech standards themselves: assuming the true value is 1.1 or close, Ill report it as machine accuracy. To get to that number, I need to calculate the absolute difference of each from the true value, and then average those differences. That should give me a +/- % - the numbers were with a machine accuracy of +/- %

  • Ill report soil replicability seperately: well do that with paired differences (we cant do standard deviations because we dont know the true values). To get a replicability %, I need to take the absolute difference between each duplicate, and then divide by the average. In theory, I should be able to distinguish between analytical variability of the costech, and analytical variability of the kmd, but Dan says I should lump them all together.
